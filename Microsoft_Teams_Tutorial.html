1. Getting Started with Teams

What is Microsoft Teams?

Microsoft Teams is a collaboration platform that combines workplace chat, video meetings, file storage, and application integration. It's part of the Microsoft 365 suite and serves as a digital hub for teamwork.

Key Benefits:

  • Unified communication (chat, calls, meetings)
  • Real-time collaboration on documents
  • Integration with Microsoft 365 apps
  • Cross-platform availability
  • Enterprise-grade security

Installation and Setup

Step 1: Download Teams

Visit teams.microsoft.com and download the desktop app, or use the web version directly in your browser.

You should see the Teams website homepage with a prominent "Download for desktop" button and options for "Use the web app instead" below it. The page will show the Teams logo and tagline "Connect and collaborate with anyone from anywhere on Teams."

Step 2: Sign In

Use your Microsoft 365 work/school account or personal Microsoft account to sign in.

The sign-in screen displays the Microsoft Teams logo at the top, with an email input field labeled "Sign in" and options for "Work or school account" and "Personal account" below. You'll also see "Sign in with a one-time code instead" link at the bottom.

Step 3: Set Up Your Profile

Add a profile picture, update your status, and configure notification preferences.

2. Understanding the Interface

Main Navigation Bar (Left Side)

The Teams interface shows a dark purple navigation bar on the left side with icons for each section. At the top is your profile picture/avatar, followed by vertically aligned icons: Activity (bell icon), Chat (message bubble), Teams (people icon), Calendar (calendar icon), Calls (phone icon), Files (folder icon), and Apps (grid icon). The currently selected section is highlighted in white.
  • Activity: View notifications, mentions, and recent activity
  • Chat: Private and group conversations
  • Teams: Access your teams and channels
  • Calendar: Schedule and join meetings
  • Calls: Make and receive phone calls
  • Files: Access shared and personal files
  • Apps: Installed third-party applications

3. Teams and Channels

Understanding Teams

A Team is a collection of people, content, and tools surrounding different projects and outcomes within an organization.

Understanding Channels

Channels are dedicated sections within a team to keep conversations organized by specific topics, projects, disciplines, or whatever works for your team.

Creating a New Team

Step 1: In the Teams section, you'll see your existing teams listed. Look for a "Create team" button or "Join or create a team" option at the bottom of the teams list.
  1. Click "Teams" in the left sidebar
  2. Click "Create team"
  3. Choose team type (Class, PLC, Staff, or Other)
  4. Enter team name and description
  5. Set privacy level (Public or Private)
  6. Add team members
Step 2-3: A dialog box appears with template options. You'll see tiles for "Create a team from scratch" and various templates like "Manage a Project," "Manage an Event," etc. Each tile shows an icon and description of the team type.

Creating a New Channel

  1. Navigate to your team
  2. Click the "..." next to the team name
  3. Select "Add channel"
  4. Enter channel name and description
  5. Choose privacy settings
  6. Click "Create"

Channel Types

  • Standard: Accessible to all team members
  • Private: Only accessible to specific members
  • Shared: Accessible to people outside your organization

4. Meetings and Video Calls

Scheduling a Meeting

Method 1: From Calendar

The Calendar view shows a monthly, weekly, or daily calendar layout. At the top right, you'll see a prominent "New meeting" button. The interface resembles Outlook calendar with time slots and existing meetings displayed as colored blocks.
  1. Click "Calendar" in the left sidebar
  2. Click "New meeting"
  3. Fill in meeting details (title, attendees, date, time)
  4. Add meeting options (lobby settings, recording permissions)
  5. Click "Send"
Steps 3-5: The meeting form opens with fields for "Add a title," "Invite people" (with @mention suggestions), date/time pickers, and a "Meeting options" link. You'll also see options for "Add channel" and meeting room booking if available.

Method 2: Meet Now

  1. Go to a channel or chat
  2. Click the video camera icon "Meet now"
  3. Choose meeting settings
  4. Click "Start meeting"

During a Meeting

Essential Controls:

During a meeting, the control bar appears at the bottom of the screen with circular buttons. From left to right: microphone icon (mute/unmute), camera icon (video on/off), share screen icon, "..." more actions menu, and red "Leave" button. Participant videos appear in a grid layout above the controls.
  • Mute/Unmute: Ctrl + Shift + M
  • Turn camera on/off: Ctrl + Shift + O
  • Share screen: Click "Share content" button
  • Chat: Click "Show conversation" button
  • Record: Click "..." ‚Üí "Start recording"
  • Breakout rooms: Available for meeting organizers

5. Chat and Messaging

Types of Conversations

  • 1:1 Chat: Private conversation between two people
  • Group Chat: Conversation with multiple people
  • Channel Conversation: Team-wide discussion in a channel

Starting a Chat

New Chat

In the Chat section, you'll see a list of recent conversations on the left panel. At the top of this list is a "New chat" button with a pencil icon. When clicked, a "To:" field appears where you can type names or email addresses, with autocomplete suggestions appearing below.
  1. Click "Chat" in the left sidebar
  2. Click "New chat" (pencil icon)
  3. Type names or email addresses
  4. Add a group name (for group chats)
  5. Start typing your message

Message Features

Rich Text Options:

  • Format text: Bold, italic, underline, highlights
  • @Mentions: Type @ followed by person's name
  • Emojis and GIFs: Click emoji button below message box
  • File attachments: Drag and drop or click attach button
  • Voice messages: Hold microphone button to record
  • Message reactions: Hover over message and click emoji

6. File Sharing and Collaboration

Sharing Files

In Chat or Channel

At the bottom of the chat window, you'll see the message composition area. Below the text box are several icons including an emoji button, @mentions, and a paperclip icon for attachments. When clicked, a menu appears with options for "Upload from this device," "Browse cloud locations," and recent files.
  1. Click the "Attach" button (paperclip icon)
  2. Choose "Upload from this device" or "Browse cloud locations"
  3. Select your file and click "Share"
  4. Choose sharing permissions

File Collaboration

  • Real-time co-authoring: Multiple people can edit simultaneously
  • Version history: Track changes and restore previous versions
  • Comments: Add comments for feedback and discussion
  • @Mentions in documents: Tag colleagues for their attention

Files Tab

Each channel has a "Files" tab where all shared files are stored and organized. Files are automatically saved to the team's SharePoint site.

üí° Pro Tip: Use the Files app in Teams to access all your files across different teams and OneDrive in one place.

7. Apps and Integrations

Popular Teams Apps

  • Planner: Task management and project tracking
  • OneNote: Digital notebooks for team collaboration
  • Power BI: Data visualization and business intelligence
  • Trello: Project management boards
  • Adobe Creative Cloud: Design and creative tools
  • GitHub: Code repository integration

Adding Apps to Teams

Add Apps to a Channel

When viewing a channel, look at the top of the main content area. You'll see tabs like "Posts," "Files," and others. At the end of the tab row is a "+" button. Clicking it opens the "Add a tab" dialog with a gallery of available apps including OneNote, Planner, Power BI, and more.
  1. Go to the desired channel
  2. Click the "+" button in the tab bar
  3. Browse or search for apps
  4. Click "Add" on your chosen app
  5. Configure app settings
  6. Click "Save"

10. Troubleshooting

Common Issues and Solutions

Audio/Video Problems

  • Check device permissions in browser/app settings
  • Test audio/video in Teams settings
  • Update device drivers
  • Close other applications using camera/microphone
  • Try using Teams in a different browser

Connection Issues

  • Check internet connection stability
  • Clear Teams cache and restart application
  • Disable VPN temporarily
  • Contact IT administrator for firewall settings

Performance Issues

  • Close unnecessary browser tabs and applications
  • Update Teams to the latest version
  • Disable hardware acceleration in Teams settings
  • Use Teams desktop app instead of web browser
